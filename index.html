What is the physics behind this?

Assumptions

In order to calculate the lean angle from the pure physic perspective, we've made some assumptions to simplify the work.

1. Design of the motorcycle

We assume that all the motorcycles are the same, and the frame of the motorcycle does not affect the lean angle.

2. Rider's skill

Rider's skill does affect the lean angle, and it cannot be calculated, so let's assume rider is skilled!

3. Road condition

We assume the road is perfectly made, flat and smooth.

OK, got it!

Physics formula

Here are two main formula to calculate the lean angle by given the speed and the turn radius

Friction formula :

$$F_f = m{v^2 \over R}$$

$$\mu N = \mu mg = m{v^2 \over R}$$

$$\mu = {v^2 \over gR}$$

Lean Angle formula :

$$ \tau_{net-0} = 0 = mgL \sin \theta - m{v^2 \over r }L \cos \theta $$

$$g \sin \theta = {v^2 \over r} \cos \theta$$

$${\sin \theta \over \cos \theta} = \tan \theta = {v^2 \over rg}$$

$$\tan \theta = \mu $$

What does that means ?

Take Aways Form Formula

Here are some observations that take away from both formulas

Tighter Turn

Tighter turn means you will lean more or slower your speed to take the turn !

Faster Speed

Faster speed means you have to lean more !

Static Friction Coefficient

Based on the formula, friction coefficient can up to 1.7 !

Speed + Turn

After simplify the formula, only speed and turn radius affect the lean angle !
